☀️ How to Choose the Right Sunscreen

🧴 Mineral vs Chemical

Mineral (zinc, titanium) sits on skin and reflects UV — gentler for sensitive skin, slight white cast. Chemical filters absorb UV — invisible finish, better for daily makeup wear. Hybrid formulas give you both.

📊 SPF Numbers Explained

SPF 30 blocks 97% of UVB. SPF 50 blocks 98%. The gap is small — what matters most is applying enough (most people apply 25% of the recommended amount) and reapplying every 2 hours.

🎨 Why Tinted SPF Matters

Regular SPF only blocks UV. Tinted formulas with iron oxides also block visible light — the wavelength that triggers melasma. If you have hyperpigmentation, a tinted SPF is non-negotiable.

⚠️ Common Mistakes

Not applying enough (use a teaspoon for face + neck), not reapplying after 2 hours, skipping SPF on cloudy days (UV penetrates clouds), and assuming makeup with SPF is sufficient protection.
