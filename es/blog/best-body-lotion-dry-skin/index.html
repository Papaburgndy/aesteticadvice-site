The Soak and Seal Method

Dermatologists' most important advice for dry body skin: apply lotion within 3 minutes of showering while your skin is still slightly damp. Don't pat completely dry — leave a thin film of water, then immediately apply moisturizer.

This "soak and seal" technique traps shower water inside the skin, dramatically boosting hydration compared to applying lotion to completely dry skin.

Preguntas Frecuentes

What ingredients should I look for in body lotion for dry skin?
You need all three moisturizer types: humectants (hyaluronic acid, glycerin, urea) that draw water in; emollients (shea butter, ceramides) that smooth and soften; and occlusives (petrolatum, dimethicone) that seal moisture in. Avoid fragrances and alcohol.
Should you apply body lotion on wet or dry skin?
Slightly damp skin, within 3 minutes of showering. Pat skin so it's not dripping, but leave some moisture. Apply lotion immediately. This "soak and seal" technique dramatically improves results.
How often should you apply body lotion for dry skin?
At minimum, once daily after showering. For very dry or eczema-prone skin, twice daily significantly improves barrier function. Focus on shins, elbows, and heels — areas with fewer oil glands.
