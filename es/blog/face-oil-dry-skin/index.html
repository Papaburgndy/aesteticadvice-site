How to Apply Face Oil Correctly

The order of application is crucial. Face oil goes last — after all serums and moisturizer. It acts as an occlusive layer, sealing everything underneath and preventing moisture evaporation (TEWL).

  • Warm 3–5 drops between palms
  • Press into skin with flat hands (don't rub)
  • Allow 60 seconds to absorb before applying makeup/SPF
  • In the morning: apply SPF on top (oil doesn't provide SPF)
💡 Pro Tip: For extremely dry skin, try facial "slugging" with an oil instead of petroleum jelly. Apply a thin layer of squalane as the final step in your evening routine to wake up with visibly plumper, more hydrated skin.

Preguntas Frecuentes

Do face oils replace moisturizer?
No — oils are occlusive (they seal moisture in) but they don't provide water-based hydration. Use: serum → moisturizer → face oil. Using oil alone can seal in dryness. Always layer it over a water-based moisturizer.
When should you apply face oil in your routine?
Last step before SPF in the morning, or as the final step in your evening routine. It seals all your other products underneath. Never apply before serums or moisturizer.
Which face oils are completely non-comedogenic?
Squalane (rating 0), hemp seed oil (0), and rosehip oil (1) are the safest options for acne-prone skin. Avoid coconut oil (4), marula oil (3–4), and wheat germ oil (5) on your face.
