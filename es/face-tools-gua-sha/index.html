Frequently Asked Questions

Does gua sha actually work for face lifting?
Gua sha provides temporary depuffing and improved lymphatic drainage, which can create a more sculpted appearance for several hours. However, it does not permanently lift facial tissue. Clinical evidence supports its ability to increase local blood circulation by up to 400% and reduce puffiness, but claims about permanent face lifting are not supported by peer-reseñaed studies.
How often should you gua sha your face?
For best results, gua sha your face 3-5 times per week for 3-5 minutes per session. Daily use is safe with gentle pressure and facial oil for glide. Always use upward and outward strokes, and never use gua sha on active acne, sunburned skin, or broken capillaries.
What is the best material for a gua sha tool?
Jade and rose quartz are the most popular, but the material matters less than shape and technique. Jade stays cooler longer (better for depuffing), rose quartz warms quickly. Stainless steel is the most hygienic since it is non-porous and easy to sanitize. Avoid plastic tools.
Can gua sha cause broken capillaries?
Yes, if used with too much pressure. Use the weight of the tool with minimal added pressure. If redness lasts more than 15 minutes, you are pressing too hard. People with rosacea or very sensitive skin should consult a dermatologist before starting gua sha.
