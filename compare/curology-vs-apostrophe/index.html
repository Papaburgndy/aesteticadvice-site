Curology — Full Review

🏆 Best for Acne
Curology
~$30/month

Curology's core product is a custom three-ingredient formula blended for your specific skin. You submit photos and a questionnaire, a licensed provider reviews your case within 24–48 hours, and prescribes a personalized combination from their ingredient library: usually tretinoin, niacinamide, and one additional active (azelaic acid, clindamycin, zinc pyrithione, or others).

The custom blend approach works well for acne — combining a retinoid with an antibiotic (clindamycin) or anti-inflammatory (niacinamide) in a single step reduces the barrier to compliance. The price point ($30/month after the free trial period) is genuinely competitive with buying these ingredients separately.

Pros
  • ✓ $30/month — most affordable prescription option
  • ✓ Free trial (pay shipping only)
  • ✓ Fast 24–48 hour response time
  • ✓ Easy formula adjustments
  • ✓ Good for first-time prescription skincare users
Cons
  • ✗ Custom blend (not individual prescriptions)
  • ✗ No spironolactone for hormonal acne
  • ✗ Provider team is NPs/PAs, not MDs
  • ✗ Less ingredient flexibility than Apostrophe

Bottom line: The best entry point for prescription skincare. Excellent for acne, budget-friendly, and low commitment with the free trial.

Apostrophe — Full Review

⭐ Best for Anti-Aging & Complex Concerns
Apostrophe
~$40–80/month

Apostrophe takes a different approach: instead of a blended formula, it prescribes individual products — which means you can get tretinoin at specific concentrations, prescription-strength azelaic acid (20%), spironolactone (for hormonal acne), or oral antibiotics, depending on your case. Consultations are handled by board-certified dermatologists rather than NPs.

For anti-aging, this matters: Apostrophe can prescribe tretinoin 0.05% or 0.1% — concentrations not available through Curology's blended approach. For hormonal acne (particularly in adult women), the ability to prescribe oral spironolactone is a major differentiator.

Pros
  • ✓ Board-certified dermatologists (not NPs)
  • ✓ Spironolactone for hormonal acne
  • ✓ Higher tretinoin concentrations available
  • ✓ Prescription-strength azelaic acid 20%
  • ✓ More clinical for complex presentations
Cons
  • ✗ More expensive ($40–80/month)
  • ✗ No free trial period
  • ✗ Slightly slower to adjust formulas
  • ✗ Larger upfront cost commitment

Bottom line: Best for anti-aging goals, hormonal acne, or anyone who wants board-certified dermatologist oversight and more prescription options.

Who Should Choose Which

Choose Curology if:
  • → Budget is a priority
  • → You want to try prescription skincare for the first time
  • → Your concern is primarily acne (not hormonal)
  • → You want frequent, easy formula adjustments
  • → You want to try before committing
Choose Apostrophe if:
  • → Anti-aging is your primary goal
  • → You have hormonal acne (spironolactone)
  • → You want MD oversight specifically
  • → You need higher tretinoin concentrations
  • → You have a complex or resistant skin condition

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Curology or Apostrophe better for acne?
Curology's combination formula (tretinoin + clindamycin or niacinamide) is highly effective for acne and more affordable. For hormonal acne specifically, Apostrophe wins — it can prescribe oral spironolactone, which addresses the hormonal root cause of adult female acne in a way no topical can.
Can you get tretinoin from both platforms?
Yes — both offer tretinoin. Curology includes it as part of their custom blend, typically at 0.025–0.1% depending on your skin. Apostrophe prescribes tretinoin as a standalone product and can reach higher concentrations (up to 0.1%) for experienced retinoid users.
Are these platforms safe?
Yes — both are licensed telehealth platforms operating under state medical board regulations. Prescriptions are written by licensed providers after reviewing your skin photos and history. Neither platform can examine your skin in person, which is a limitation — if you have a condition requiring in-person assessment (suspicious moles, severe cystic acne, etc.), see a dermatologist directly.
How long until you see results?
Tretinoin from either platform typically shows visible improvement in 8–12 weeks, with full results at 6 months. Initial "purging" (temporary breakout) is common in weeks 2–6 — this is normal and indicates the tretinoin is working to turn over congested pores.
