Use Both — They Do Different Things

The Ordinary wins for active treatment serums (vitamin C, niacinamide, exfoliants, retinol). CeraVe wins for cleansers, moisturizers, and barrier repair products. The ideal budget routine combines both brands rather than choosing one.

Brand Philosophy: Fundamentally Different Approaches

Understanding why these brands exist explains what they each do best:

The Ordinary (by DECIEM) was founded on radical price transparency — showing that active ingredients don't need to cost $100+. They sell single-ingredient or minimally-formulated products at production-cost pricing. This is perfect when you know what active you want. It's overwhelming when you don't.

CeraVe was developed with dermatologist input to create complete skincare formulas — not single ingredients. Their philosophy is barrier repair first: every product contains the three essential ceramides (1, 3, 6-II) and hyaluronic acid. CeraVe is designed for people who want products that work reliably without skincare knowledge.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is The Ordinary better than CeraVe?
Neither is universally better — they serve different functions. The Ordinary excels at active treatment serums (niacinamide, vitamin C, retinol, exfoliants) at unbeatable prices. CeraVe excels at cleansers, moisturizers, and barrier repair. The best routine uses both brands for what each does best.
Can beginners use The Ordinary?
Yes, but start with 2–3 products maximum. The best beginner The Ordinary products: Niacinamide 10% + Zinc (nearly zero irritation risk), Hyaluronic Acid 2% + B5 (pure hydration), and Natural Moisturizing Factors (simple moisturizer). Avoid the AHA/BHA peel and high-concentration vitamin C until you understand how your skin reacts to actives.
Does The Ordinary use the same ingredients as luxury brands?
Frequently, yes. Niacinamide, hyaluronic acid, retinol, alpha arbutin, and glycolic acid are commodity ingredients available to any brand. The Ordinary simply charges close to raw material cost rather than adding a large marketing markup. Where luxury brands genuinely differ: proprietary delivery systems, stabilization technology (important for vitamin C and retinol), and elegant textures that make compliance easier.
