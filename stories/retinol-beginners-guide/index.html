Retinol: A Beginner's Guide

Master the most effective anti-aging ingredient
What is Retinol?

The Gold Standard

Retinol is a vitamin A derivative proven to reduce fine lines, improve skin texture, and boost collagen production.
How It Works

The Science

Retinol converts to retinoic acid in your skin, stimulating cell turnover and collagen synthesis for visible results.
Percentages

Strength Guide

0.025-0.03% (beginner) → 0.05-0.1% (intermediate) → 0.25-0.3% (advanced). Start low, go slow.
When to Start

The Right Time

Begin retinol once your skin is healthy. Introduce it gradually—1-2x per week at first, building tolerance over weeks.
Side Effects

The Adjustment Period

Dryness, redness, and peeling are normal. Use a hydrating moisturizer and sunscreen. Effects subside in 2-4 weeks.
Layering Tips

Application Rules

Wait 20 minutes after cleansing. Apply to damp skin. Wait before moisturizer. Never mix with vitamin C or AHAs.
