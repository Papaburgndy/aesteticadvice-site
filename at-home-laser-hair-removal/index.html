🔬 IPL vs Laser: What's the Difference?

💡 IPL (Intense Pulsed Light)

Broad-spectrum light that targets melanin in hair follicles. Safer for beginners, covers larger areas quickly, but requires more sessions (6–12) for permanent reduction. Works on skin types I–IV.

🔴 Diode Laser

Single-wavelength focused beam (808nm) that reaches deeper into follicles. More effective per session, faster permanent results, but more expensive and uncomfortable. Only the Tria 4X offers this at home.

🎨 Skin Type Matters

Both technologies work on the contrast between hair and skin. Darker skin tones (Fitzpatrick V–VI) face absorption risks. Always check the device's Fitzpatrick scale compatibility before purchasing.

📅 Consistency Is Key

Hair grows in cycles — only anagen (active growth) phase hairs are affected. You need treatments every 2 weeks for 8–12 sessions to catch all follicles in the right phase. Skip sessions and you restart the clock.

At-Home Device FAQs

Most people see 80–90% hair reduction after 8–12 sessions, spaced 2 weeks apart. The first 4 sessions show the most dramatic reduction. Touch-up sessions every 6–12 months maintain results. Hormonal areas (chin, upper lip) may need more frequent maintenance.
FDA-cleared IPL devices (Braun, Ulike, Philips Lumea) are safe when used correctly. Key safety rules: never use on Fitzpatrick V–VI skin tones, avoid tattooed skin, don't use over moles, always shave before treating, and never treat the eye area. Read your device manual completely before first use.
Clinical evidence supports red light (630–660nm) for stimulating collagen production and reducing inflammation. Blue light (415nm) kills P. acnes bacteria. Medical-grade devices (Omnilux, CurrentBody) with correct wavelengths and energy densities show real results. Consumer toys with incorrect wavelengths do not.
Dermarollers use a rolling motion with fixed needles — cheaper but less precise, with potential for tearing rather than puncturing. Dermapens use a motorized vertical stamping motion — more hygienic, consistent depth, and safer for at-home use. We recommend dermapens for home use. See our full comparison.
