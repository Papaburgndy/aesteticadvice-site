Double Cleansing: The Korean Skincare Method That Dermatologists Now Recommend

By AesteticAdvice Editors · Updated March 2026 · ⏱ 9 min read · ✅ Expert Reviewed
Disclosure: AesteticAdvice may earn a commission from Amazon and other retailers when you use our links — at no extra cost to you. We never accept payment for reviews or rankings.

Double cleansing is one of the most widely-practiced habits in Korean skincare — and one of the most misunderstood in Western routines. Done correctly, it removes SPF and makeup that water-based cleansers miss while keeping your skin barrier intact. Done incorrectly, it strips your skin dry.

✅ Why Double Cleansing Works: Sunscreen, makeup, and sebum are oil-based. Water-based cleansers can't fully dissolve them — you need like to dissolve like. An oil cleanser breaks down oily residue, and a gentle water-based cleanser removes the oil cleanser itself, leaving skin genuinely clean without over-stripping.

Step 1: The Oil Cleanser

Oil cleansers come in three formats: cleansing oils (liquid), cleansing balms (solid), and micellar waters (gentler, for sensitive skin). All work on the same principle — oil-based ingredients dissolve oil-based residue.

Apply the oil cleanser to dry skin, massage for 60 seconds, then add water to emulsify it into a milky texture before rinsing. The emulsification step is critical — if you don't do it, you'll leave oily residue on your skin.

Step 2: The Water-Based Cleanser

After rinsing the oil cleanser, apply a gentle water-based cleanser to remove any remaining residue, sweat, and water-soluble impurities. The key is gentle — you've already done the heavy lifting with the oil cleanser. A harsh foaming cleanser as Step 2 defeats the purpose.

Look for pH-balanced cleansers in the 4.5–6.5 range, without sulfates (SLS/SLES), fragrance, or drying alcohols.

Who Should Double Cleanse?

  • Anyone wearing SPF daily (which should be everyone)
  • Anyone wearing foundation or concealer
  • Oily or acne-prone skin — double cleansing prevents sebum + SPF buildup that clogs pores
  • Anyone who has active ingredients (retinol, AHAs) not working as well as expected — incomplete cleansing reduces absorption

Who Can Skip It?

If you wear minimal SPF, no makeup, and have very dry or sensitive skin — a single gentle cleanser is fine. Over-cleansing causes more problems than under-cleansing for sensitive skin types.

💡 Evening Only: Double cleansing is an evening routine only. In the morning, your face is already clean from the night before — a gentle rinse or light water-based cleanse is sufficient. Morning double cleansing is unnecessary and strips your barrier.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does double cleansing cause dry skin?
It shouldn't, if you choose the right products. A nourishing oil cleanser followed by a hydrating water-based cleanser is often less drying than a single harsh foaming cleanser. Dryness from double cleansing usually means the water-based cleanser is too stripping.
Can oily skin double cleanse?
Yes — oily skin often benefits the most from double cleansing because it clears the oil and SPF buildup that feeds acne bacteria. Use a lightweight cleansing oil (not a balm) and a gentle gel cleanser as your second step.
Can I use micellar water as my first step?
Micellar water is a gentler alternative to oil cleansers for sensitive skin. It's effective for light SPF and minimal makeup but may not fully remove heavy-duty sunscreen or full-coverage foundation. If your micellar water still leaves residue on a cotton pad after the first wipe, use an oil cleanser instead.
