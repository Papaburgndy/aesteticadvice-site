Curology vs Nava MD (2026): Which Prescription Skincare Service Is Worth It?

By AesteticAdvice Editors · Updated March 29, 2026 · ⏱ 8 min read · ✅ Expert Reviewed
Disclosure: This article is for informational purposes only and does not constitute medical advice. Consult a licensed provider before starting any prescription treatment.

Both Curology and Nava MD let you skip the dermatologist waiting room and get prescription-strength skincare — including tretinoin — through an online consultation. Both are legitimate, widely used telehealth services. But they work differently, cost differently, and are better suited to different skin goals.

This comparison cuts through the marketing so you can pick the right one for your skin in under 10 minutes.

🏆 Best for Anti-Aging & Hyperpigmentation
Nava MD
Tretinoin-first approach with optional hydroquinone and azelaic acid. More affordable at $20–$30/month. Best for users focused on wrinkles, fine lines, dark spots, or melasma.

Service Overview

Both services follow the same basic model: complete a skin questionnaire + photo consultation, get matched with a licensed provider (NP, PA, or dermatologist), receive a customized prescription shipped to your door, and follow up as needed online.

Curology was founded in 2014 and popularized the custom-blended prescription skincare model. They combine multiple active ingredients (tretinoin, niacinamide, azelaic acid, clindamycin, etc.) into a single compounded formula unique to you.

Nava MD takes a more conventional approach: they prescribe well-established formulations (tretinoin cream, azelaic acid, hydroquinone) individually rather than blending everything together. Think of it as closer to what a traditional dermatologist would prescribe, just accessible online.

Round-by-Round

Price
Winner: Nava MD
Nava MD typically runs $20–$30/month for a prescription formula. Curology runs $30–$40/month. Over a year, that's a $120–$240 difference. Both are far cheaper than traditional dermatology visits + pharmacy co-pays.
Ingredient Range
Winner: Nava MD
Nava MD can prescribe hydroquinone — a prescription-strength tyrosinase inhibitor that is the most effective treatment for melasma and significant hyperpigmentation. Curology cannot include hydroquinone. For users with pigmentation as a primary concern, this is a meaningful difference.
Acne Treatment
Winner: Curology
Curology's blended formula approach shines for acne. They can combine tretinoin (comedolytic) + clindamycin (antibacterial) + niacinamide (anti-inflammatory) in one bottle. Nava MD is more tretinoin-focused and less optimized for complex, active acne cases.
Formula Customization
Winner: Curology
Curology's compounding approach means your formula is uniquely yours and can be adjusted based on how your skin responds. Nava MD works with standard formulations, which are well-proven but less granularly adjustable.
Anti-Aging Focus
Winner: Nava MD
Nava MD treats tretinoin as the centerpiece — you know exactly what concentration you're getting. Combined with optional hydroquinone and azelaic acid, it's a more targeted anti-aging and brightening stack than Curology's blended approach.
Getting Started
Winner: Curology
Curology's 30-day trial for just $5 shipping is the best low-risk entry point in the category. You can test whether your formula works before committing. Nava MD has no trial offer — you start with a full subscription.

Who Should Choose Which

Choose Nava MD if: Anti-aging is your primary goal, you have significant hyperpigmentation or melasma, you want the lowest monthly cost, or you prefer prescription-standard formulations over compounded blends.
Choose Curology if: You have active acne alongside other skin concerns, you want everything in one custom bottle, or you want to try a service risk-free with a 30-day trial before committing.
Important: Both services are supplements to — not replacements for — in-person dermatology. If you have a complex skin condition, significant scarring, or have not responded to prior treatments, see a board-certified dermatologist in person.

Both Services Give You Tretinoin Access

The core value of both Curology and Nava MD is the same: access to tretinoin without a traditional dermatologist appointment, at a fraction of the cost. Tretinoin — the prescription-strength retinoid with 40+ years of clinical evidence — is the most effective topical anti-aging and acne ingredient available. A dermatologist visit + tretinoin prescription can cost $150–$300+. Both services deliver it for $20–$40/month including ongoing provider support.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Curology or Nava MD better?
Curology is better for acne and users who want a highly customized multi-active blend. Nava MD is better for anti-aging, hyperpigmentation, and users who want the most affordable tretinoin access with optional hydroquinone.
Does Curology prescribe tretinoin?
Yes — tretinoin is one of the ingredients Curology providers can include in your custom formula. It's blended with other actives rather than prescribed as a standalone product.
How much does Nava MD cost?
Nava MD typically starts at $20–$30/month including provider consultation, prescription, and shipping. This makes it one of the most affordable telehealth prescription skincare services available.
Can I get hydroquinone online without a doctor visit?
Yes, through telehealth services like Nava MD. Hydroquinone above 2% is prescription-only in the US. Nava MD providers can assess your skin and prescribe it as part of your treatment plan if appropriate.
