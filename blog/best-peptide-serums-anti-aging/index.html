Peptides are short chains of amino acids (the building blocks of proteins) that act as cellular messengers. When applied topically, certain peptides signal fibroblasts to upregulate collagen and elastin production — essentially sending a "repair" message that your skin responds to by building new structural proteins.

The clinical evidence is solid but takes longer to manifest than retinol — typically 3–6 months of daily use for visible results. The trade-off: zero irritation, no sun sensitivity, safe during pregnancy, and compatible with every other skincare ingredient.

Key Peptide Types in Skincare

  • Signal peptides (Matrixyl 3000, Argireline): Signal fibroblasts to produce collagen. Most relevant for anti-aging.
  • Carrier peptides (GHK-Cu, copper peptides): Deliver trace minerals for collagen synthesis. GHK-Cu has strong wound healing evidence.
  • Neurotransmitter-inhibiting peptides (Argireline/acetyl hexapeptide-3): Mildly reduce muscle contractions, targeting expression lines.

FAQ

Are peptides better than retinol for anti-aging?
Not better — different. Retinol is more potent and works faster, but causes irritation during adjustment and requires careful use. Peptides are gentler, produce gradual results, and are suitable for people who can't use retinol. The ideal routine uses both: retinol at night when tolerated, peptides morning and/or evening for additional collagen support.
Can I use peptides with vitamin C and retinol?
Yes — peptides are compatible with virtually every other skincare ingredient. One note: very low pH environments (like a properly formulated vitamin C serum at pH 2.5–3) may reduce peptide stability. If layering, apply peptides after vitamin C has fully absorbed.
How long until peptides work?
Collagen production from signal peptide stimulation takes 3–6 months to show as visible skin improvement. Patience is essential — peptides are a long-game investment compared to the more immediate (but more irritating) results from retinol.
