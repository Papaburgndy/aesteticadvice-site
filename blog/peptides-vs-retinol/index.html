Who Should Use Peptides

  • Sensitive skin that can't tolerate retinol
  • Pregnant or nursing women (retinol is contraindicated)
  • Mid-20s to early 30s just starting anti-aging
  • Anyone who wants a daytime anti-aging serum (peptides are photostable)

Who Should Use Retinol

  • Anyone dealing with visible fine lines, texture, or sun damage
  • Acne-prone skin (retinol also fights breakouts)
  • Committed users willing to go through an adjustment period
  • Those seeking the most clinically proven anti-aging results

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you use peptides and retinol together?
Yes — they're a powerful combination. Use retinol at night, peptides in the morning and/or evening. If layering on the same night, apply retinol first, wait 30 minutes, then apply peptides. They work synergistically for comprehensive anti-aging.
Are peptides better than retinol for sensitive skin?
Yes. Peptides are significantly gentler — no purging, peeling, or adjustment period. They signal collagen production without forcing cell turnover. The tradeoff is slower, more subtle results. Perfect for sensitive skin types or beginners.
When should you start using retinol?
Most dermatologists recommend late 20s to early 30s as a preventive measure. Starting peptides in your mid-20s is perfectly fine. For 40+ with significant fine lines, use both together for best results.
