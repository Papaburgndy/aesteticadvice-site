Hims vs Hers: Same Company, Different Products — Which Is Right for You?

Published: 2026-02-26 8 min read

The Confusing Truth: Hims and Hers Are (Mostly) the Same Company

When Hims & Hers Health rebranded their website structure, they created a confusing market situation. "Hims" and "Hers" look like competitors, but they're actually sister companies under the same parent. It's like how Toyota owns Lexus—different branding, different markets, same underlying company.

The question isn't really "which is better?" It's "which is designed for your gender and health concerns?" Because that's the only substantive difference.

The Corporate Structure (Why This Matters)

In 2021, Hims Inc. merged with Hers to form Hims & Hers Health Inc. They now operate as integrated subsidiaries rather than competitors. This means:

  • Shared telehealth infrastructure and medical licensing.
  • Same parent company investment and R&D budget.
  • Same quality standards (both are regulated by the same FDA oversight).
  • Different marketing, different product focus, different user experience.

So when comparing them, you're comparing two divisions of the same company, not two companies competing.

Product Portfolio: The Real Difference

Hims Focus: Men's Health

  • Hair loss: Finasteride (Propecia), minoxidil (Rogaine), topical combo treatments
  • Erectile dysfunction: Sildenafil (Viagra), tadalafil (Cialis), custom formulations
  • Skincare: Tretinoin, niacinamide, vitamin C serums
  • Sexual health: Premature ejaculation treatments, testosterone optimization
  • General wellness: Cold/flu care, mental health screening, antacids

Hers Focus: Women's Health

  • Hair loss: Minoxidil (Rogaine), hair wellness vitamins, topical treatments
  • Hormonal contraception: Birth control pills, patches, rings (major differentiator)
  • Skincare: Tretinoin, vitamin C, salicylic acid, hydroquinone for dark spots
  • Menopause support: Hormone replacement therapy consultations
  • Women's wellness: Vaginal health products, period delay treatments
  • General health: UTI treatment, yeast infection medication

Notice: Hims doesn't market erectile dysfunction to Hers, and Hers doesn't market birth control to Hims. It's market segmentation, not product exclusivity.

Product Quality: Essentially Identical

Both use the same pharmaceutical sources. A finasteride prescription from Hims is identical to minoxidil from Hers—same medication, same dosage, same maker. The difference is packaging, labeling, and marketing.

If you need finasteride (hair loss), both services can prescribe it. If you need tretinoin (anti-aging), both can do it. The active ingredient is identical.

Quality scores: Both are 8/10. Not perfect (customer service can be slow), but legitimate telehealth services with licensed providers.

Pricing: Nearly Identical (With Slight Variations)

Hims Pricing:

  • Consultation: $30-40 (one-time, required for Rx medications)
  • Hair loss (finasteride): $40/month generic, $60+ for brand-name
  • Erectile dysfunction: $40-60/month depending on medication
  • Skincare: $30-80/month depending on customization

Hers Pricing:

  • Consultation: $30-50 (varies by service)
  • Hair loss (minoxidil): $35-50/month
  • Birth control: $15-30/month (depending on pill type)
  • Skincare: $30-80/month (same range as Hims)

Prices are competitive between them. Occasionally one offers better pricing on specific products, but generally within 10% of each other.

Winner: Roughly tied.** If birth control is your primary need, Hers might offer slight savings. If you need multiple services, both bundle similarly.

User Experience: Slightly Different Feels

Hims branding: Positioned as a high-tech "bro-friendly" platform. Marketing emphasizes performance (sexual performance, hair retention, gym recovery). Website is clean but masculinely styled.

Hers branding: Positioned as female-focused wellness. Marketing emphasizes holistic health (contraception, menopause support, general wellness). Website uses softer design language.

Neither is objectively better—it's purely aesthetic preference. If you feel more comfortable in a "masculine-coded" or "feminine-coded" digital space, that might sway your choice. But the backend service is fundamentally the same.

Prescription Range: Hers Has Slight Advantage for Women

Hers can prescribe birth control, which Hims cannot (because it's primarily marketed to women, though some non-cisgender individuals use it). This is Hers' biggest concrete advantage—it's a one-stop shop for contraception + skincare + wellness.

Hims' advantage is sexual performance products (ED, premature ejaculation), which Hers doesn't emphasize (though doesn't explicitly exclude).

For general telehealth (non-gender-specific), they're equal.

Customer Service: Both Middling

Both get complaints about slow response times (24-48 hour waits for provider contact). Both have OK app interfaces but sometimes glitchy experiences. Neither stands out as exceptional.

Score: 6.5/10 for both.

Medical Provider Quality: Identical Standards

Both use licensed medical doctors and nurse practitioners. Both require legitimate consultations (not just automated prescriptions). Both have refill protocols and follow-up care requirements.

The doctors on Hims aren't different from those on Hers—some providers actually work for both platforms.

The Real Choice: Who Should Use Each?

Use Hims if:

  • You're a man seeking hair loss treatment or ED medication.
  • You want sexual performance optimization.
  • You prefer masculine branding and messaging.
  • You need skincare (tretinoin, vitamin C) as a standalone service.

Use Hers if:

  • You're a woman seeking birth control (Hers' biggest unique value).
  • You want contraception + skincare in one platform.
  • You're interested in menopause support or women-specific wellness.
  • You prefer feminine branding and messaging.

Either works if:

  • You need generic prescriptions (hair loss, skincare) regardless of your gender.
  • You value one-stop shopping for telehealth.
  • Cost is your primary concern (both competitive).

The Verdict: Not Really Competitors

The honest truth: Hims and Hers aren't competitors in the traditional sense. They're market-segmented divisions of the same company. If you're a woman needing birth control + skincare, Hers is your obvious choice (it's optimized for that). If you're a man needing hair loss + ED treatment, Hims is your obvious choice.

For non-gender-specific products (skincare, general wellness), either works equally well. Choose based on branding preference and which platform feels more comfortable to you psychologically.

The quality is functionally equivalent. The prices are functionally equivalent. The difference is positioning and product range. This isn't a "one is better" situation; it's "one is designed for you."
