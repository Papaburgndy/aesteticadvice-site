The Tretinoin Purge: What's Normal vs. What's Not

The purge is real, it's temporary, and it means the tretinoin is working. Here's how to tell purging from a true breakout reaction:

  • Normal purge: Breakouts in your usual acne-prone zones, beginning 2–6 weeks after starting, resolving within 8–10 weeks
  • Not a purge: New breakouts in areas you never break out, severe cystic acne, breakouts beginning after 10+ weeks, significant redness spreading beyond application sites
⚠️ Key Rule: If you're not sure it's purging, see your prescribing dermatologist. Never increase your tretinoin frequency or concentration to "speed through" the purge — this dramatically worsens irritation without improving results.

How to Minimize Side Effects

The Sandwich Method

The most effective technique for reducing irritation: moisturizer → wait 30 minutes → tretinoin → wait 30 minutes → moisturizer. The moisturizer buffer reduces irritation significantly without meaningfully reducing efficacy according to multiple clinical studies.

Start Slow — Very Slow

  • Week 1–2: 1x per week only
  • Week 3–4: 2x per week
  • Week 5–6: 3x per week
  • Week 7+: Every other night or as tolerated

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take to see results from tretinoin?
Texture and pore improvements: 4–6 weeks. Significant fine line and hyperpigmentation improvement: 3–6 months. Full collagen remodeling results: 6–12 months. The purge phase (weeks 2–8) is followed by the best results — don't quit during this phase.
What is the tretinoin purge and how long does it last?
A temporary breakout worsening as tretinoin surfaces clogged pores. Begins weeks 2–6, lasts 4–8 weeks, then resolves. Minimize by starting slowly (1–2x/week) and using heavy moisturizer. Normal purging is in usual acne zones; new areas or breakouts after 10 weeks suggest a reaction.
Can you use moisturizer with tretinoin?
Essential. Use the sandwich method: moisturizer → 30 min → tretinoin → 30 min → moisturizer. This significantly reduces irritation. CeraVe Moisturizing Cream and COSRX Snail Mucin are the top dermatologist recommendations to pair with tretinoin.
