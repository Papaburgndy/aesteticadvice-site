🧪 How to Choose the Right Serum

🎯 Identify Your Primary Concern

Vitamin C and niacinamide target hyperpigmentation and uneven tone. Retinol and peptides address fine lines and firmness. Hyaluronic acid is pure hydration. Pick your biggest concern and build from there — don't layer too many actives at once.

📊 Check the Percentage

Concentration matters. Vitamin C needs 10–20% L-ascorbic acid to work. Niacinamide is effective at 2–10%. Too low and you won't see results; too high and you risk irritation. Look for evidence-backed concentrations, not just marketing language.

🧬 Look at the pH

Vitamin C works best at pH 2.5–3.5 — very acidic. Applying it after a high-pH toner reduces absorption. Similarly, AHAs and BHAs need low pH to exfoliate. Check formulation pH when layering actives.

⚠️ Don't Over-Layer

Using too many active serums simultaneously causes irritation and barrier damage. A solid starting routine: cleanser → HA serum → moisturizer → SPF. Add one active at a time and give each 4–6 weeks before evaluating results.
