Adapalene is a third-generation synthetic retinoid — more specifically, it's a retinoic acid receptor agonist that works directly without needing skin conversion. This makes it more stable and less irritating than regular retinol, while being significantly more effective. Originally sold as prescription-only Differin, it became OTC in 2016. At $14, it's the best value in all of skincare. FDA approved for acne, but decades of evidence also support its anti-aging benefits.

Pros

  • FDA-approved — proven efficacy
  • Works directly (no skin conversion)
  • Less irritating than tretinoin
  • Unbeatable value at $14

Cons

  • Still requires adjustment period
  • Best for acne + texture, slower for wrinkles vs. tretinoin
Bottom line: Start here. Differin is the best entry point into retinoids — prescription efficacy, OTC availability, and a price that makes consistent use easy.

⚗️ How to Choose the Right Retinoid

🎯 Start Low, Go Slow

Begin with 0.025% retinol or adapalene 1–2x per week. Increase frequency over 6–8 weeks as your skin adjusts. Jumping to high concentrations immediately causes unnecessary irritation that slows progress, not speeds it.

📊 Retinoid Strength Ladder

From weakest to strongest: retinyl esters → retinaldehyde → retinol (OTC) → adapalene → tretinoin (Rx) → tazarotene (Rx). Each step up delivers faster results with more potential for irritation.

🧴 The Sandwich Method

Moisturizer → wait 20 min → retinoid → wait 30 min → moisturizer. This buffer technique dramatically reduces irritation without meaningfully reducing efficacy, according to clinical studies.

☀️ Always Use SPF

Retinoids increase photosensitivity significantly. Apply SPF 30+ every single morning when using any retinoid — without exception. Skipping SPF with retinol causes the hyperpigmentation you're trying to treat.
