🧬 How to Choose a Hair Loss Treatment

📋 Identify the Cause First

Androgenetic alopecia (pattern baldness) responds to DHT-blocking and minoxidil. Telogen effluvium (stress/nutritional shedding) often resolves with lifestyle changes. Alopecia areata requires a dermatologist. Treating the wrong type wastes time and money.

⏱️ Start Early

Hair loss treatments work best on early-stage thinning. Once a follicle is completely miniaturized and dormant (typically after 5+ years), it cannot be reactivated. The window for regrowth is limited — starting earlier means better outcomes.

🔄 Combine Treatments

The highest-evidence protocol combines finasteride + minoxidil ± ketoconazole shampoo. Each addresses a different mechanism. Adding LLLT as a third layer further improves outcomes. No single treatment is as effective as combination therapy.

📊 Track Progress Objectively

Take monthly photos under identical lighting conditions. Hair growth is gradual — comparing week-to-week is discouraging. Give any treatment 6 full months before evaluating. Initial shedding in weeks 1–3 is normal with minoxidil and indicates the treatment is working.

Hair Loss Treatment FAQs

Minoxidil works for approximately 60–70% of people with androgenetic alopecia. It's most effective in people under 40 with hair loss duration under 5 years. It doesn't work on completely bald areas where follicles are already dead. Topical 5% works better than 2% for most men; women should use 2% (the FDA-approved female dose) unless prescribed higher by a dermatologist.
Clinical studies show sexual side effects (reduced libido, erectile dysfunction) in approximately 2–3% of men using finasteride. These effects are reversible upon discontinuation in the vast majority of cases. Post-finasteride syndrome is reported anecdotally but remains poorly understood. The decision involves weighing a ~2–3% risk of side effects against 83% likelihood of stopping hair loss progression.
Finasteride: hair loss stabilization in 3–6 months, regrowth visible at 6–12 months. Minoxidil: initial shedding (normal) in weeks 1–3, stabilization at 4–6 months, visible regrowth at 6–12 months. LLLT devices: 4–6 months to see hair count improvements. All treatments require 12 months of consistent use for full evaluation.
